Chiang Kai-Shek

Chiang Kai-Shek, or Jiang JieShi, birth name Jiang Rui Yuan, was a famous political character, former president of Whampoa Military Academy (Huang Pu Jun), former president of the Republic of China, and Commander in chief of the Chinese Nationalist Revolution army. Jiang was an impactful political character who led the Northern Expedition, a military campaign to unify China after the Xinhai Revolution. In 1925, Chiang became the leader of ROC after Sun Yat-sen’s death. In 1949, Chiang Kai-shek retreated to Taiwan with his army and government to plan for a retake of mainland China from the CCP. In 1975, Jiang passed away due to a heart attack in Taipei, Taiwan.

Mao ZeDong

Mao ZeDong was a Marxist, a revolutionist, a strategist, a leader of the Chinese Communist Party, and a leader of the Chinese People's Liberation Army. A Marxism-Leninism practitioner in China as a late modern period Chinese patriot, Mao carried in a generation that would dramatically transform the Chinese people's fortunes and the country's perspective of the world. After leading the Long March, Mao’s position as chairman stabilized. In 1949, Mao announced the establishment of the Chinese Communist Party. In 1966, Mao launched his infamous Chinese Cultural Revolution. In 1776, Mao passed away due to a series of heart attacks.

Alliances, Impacts, and Significance

Jiang JieShi and Mao ZeDong, two of the most prominent historical figures of the twentieth century, had conflicting convictions and values. Jiang believed in nationalisj, was anti-communist and anti-capitalist, while Mao believed in nationalism in his early years, he became a follower of Marxism–Leninism, Communism, and was anti-imperialist. Their various approaches to governing China had a tremendous effect on China's development and improvement up to the current day.
